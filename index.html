❤ Our Story

Why Baaki Hai exists.

Built from the inside
"This platform is what should have existed then."

This platform was built by someone who knows what it feels like from the inside.

The years of preparation that quietly become your entire identity. The isolation that grows so gradually you don't notice until the friends are gone. The specific exhaustion of giving everything to something — and still falling short. The family pressure that sits alongside every study session like an uninvited guest. The moment you realise you have been postponing living until after the result — and that the result keeps moving.

The founder of Baaki Hai came through all of that. Not out the other side with a rank — but through it, with something more difficult: the clarity that the journey itself had value, that the knowledge was real, that leaving a path is not the same as failing, and that none of it should have been carried alone.

There was the exhaustion of watching yourself give everything sincerely — and still being told it wasn't enough. The toxic relationship with preparation that you can't name while you're in it. The delayed gratification that starts as discipline and slowly becomes self-harm. The unprocessed losses — personal, professional, relational — that get quietly folded into the preparation because stopping to grieve feels like giving up.

And eventually — the pivot. Not a clean decision. An accumulation of small acknowledgements that a different life was possible. That the years of studying geopolitics, public policy, history, economics — that knowledge didn't disappear. It just needed a different address.

The founder hasn't yet achieved what they originally set out to. But they built this. And that feels like the right kind of beginning.

What Baaki Hai believes
01
You are not your rank. Your worth was never determined by a civil services exam — not once, not ever.
02
Rest is preparation. Delayed gratification taken too far becomes self-destruction. You are allowed to live now.
03
The knowledge is real. Years of studying policy, geopolitics, history — that doesn't need a certificate to have value.
04
Leaving is not failure. Walking away from a toxic relationship — even with an exam — takes more courage than staying.
05
Community heals. The isolation of preparation is a design flaw, not an inevitability. You should never carry this alone.
06
Picture abhi baaki hai. Whatever happened — the story isn't finished. There is more ahead than you can see right now.
